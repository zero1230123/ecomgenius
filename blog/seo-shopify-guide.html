The Ultimate Shopify SEO Checklist: Rank Higher & Get Free Traffic (2026)

18 min readMay 7, 2026

Paid ads are getting more expensive every year. Facebook CPMs are up 30% since 2024. But Google organic traffic? It's free, compounding, and gets better with time.

This checklist covers everything you need to rank your Shopify store higher on Google — from quick wins to advanced strategies.

1. On-Page SEO Essentials

  • ☐
    Meta Title (60 chars max) — Include your primary keyword + a benefit/hook. Example: "Wireless Earbuds Pro X3 — 36hr Battery, Noise Cancelling | Free Shipping"
  • ☐
    Meta Description (155 chars max) — Write a compelling pitch with a CTA. Include your primary keyword naturally.
  • ☐
    URL Structure — Keep URLs short and keyword-rich: /products/wireless-earbuds-pro not /products/prod-38291-v2
  • ☐
    H1 Tag — One H1 per page = your product name with primary keyword.
  • ☐
    Product Description (300+ words) — Google needs text to understand your page. Short descriptions rank poorly.

2. Image Optimization

  • ☐
    Alt Text on Every Image — Describe what's in the image with keywords: "Black wireless earbuds with charging case on marble table"
  • ☐
    Compress Images — Use WebP format. Keep images under 200KB. Use tools like TinyPNG or Shopify's built-in compression.
  • ☐
    Descriptive File Names — Rename IMG_3829.jpg to wireless-earbuds-pro-x3-black.webp

3. Technical SEO

  • ☐
    Mobile-Friendly — 70%+ of e-commerce traffic is mobile. Test with Google's Mobile-Friendly Test tool.
  • ☐
    Site Speed — Target under 3 seconds load time. Remove unused apps, optimize images, use a fast theme.
  • ☐
    SSL Certificate — Shopify includes this by default. Make sure your URL shows https://
  • ☐
    XML Sitemap — Shopify auto-generates this at yourstore.com/sitemap.xml. Submit it to Google Search Console.
  • ☐
    Fix Broken Links — Use Screaming Frog or a free tool to find and redirect 404 pages.

4. Content Strategy

  • ☐
    Blog Posts — Write 2-4 blog posts per month targeting long-tail keywords in your niche.
  • ☐
    FAQ Sections — Add FAQ schema to product pages. Google loves showing these as rich snippets.
  • ☐
    Collection Page Descriptions — Most sellers leave these blank. Write 200+ words for each collection page.

5. Keyword Research (Quick Method)

You don't need expensive tools. Here's a free method:

  • Google Autocomplete: Type your product name in Google and see what suggestions appear.
  • Amazon Search Bar: Amazon's autocomplete shows what buyers actually search for.
  • "People Also Ask": These Google boxes reveal exact questions your customers have.
  • Competitor Titles: Look at what top-ranking competitors use in their meta titles.

6. Link Building (Simple Tactics)

  • Get listed in directories: Submit your store to relevant product directories and "best of" lists.
  • Supplier links: Ask your suppliers to link to your store as an authorized retailer.
  • Guest posts: Write for blogs in your niche and include a link back to your store.
  • Social profiles: Link to your store from all social media profiles.

7. Schema Markup (Rich Snippets)

Schema markup helps Google show star ratings, prices, and availability directly in search results. This can increase your click-through rate by 30%+.

Shopify themes often include basic Product schema. Use the Google Rich Results Test to verify your pages have proper markup.
